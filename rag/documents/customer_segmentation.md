# InsightPilot AI Customer Segmentation Guide

## Purpose

Customer segmentation groups customers based on purchasing behavior, engagement, and business value.

Segments help the business design targeted retention and marketing strategies.

## High Value Customers

High value customers generate significant lifetime revenue and demonstrate consistent purchasing behavior.

These customers should receive loyalty campaigns, personalized recommendations, and retention attention.

## Loyal Customers

Loyal customers place orders frequently and maintain a long-term relationship with the business.

Purchase frequency and customer lifetime duration are important indicators of loyalty.

## At Risk Customers

At risk customers show signs of reduced engagement.

Common indicators include:

- High days since last order.
- Declining purchase frequency.
- Reduced website activity.
- Increased support interactions.
- Elevated churn probability.

At risk customers should be considered for retention campaigns.

## Churned Customers

A churned customer has a churn label of 1.

These customers have met the defined inactivity criteria used by the churn process.

## New Customers

New customers have a relatively short customer lifetime and limited purchase history.

Their behavior should be monitored as more order and engagement data becomes available.

## Segmentation Usage

Customer segments may be used in dashboards, retention campaigns, business analysis, and machine learning workflows.

Customer segmentation should use validated customer and transaction data.
